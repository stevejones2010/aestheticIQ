## Why credential verification matters

In the UK aesthetic market, the practitioner is the commercial unit, not just the clinic. A clinic with three injectable practitioners and one prescriber operates very differently from a clinic with one nurse-led practitioner. For pharma sales teams, distributors, and regulatory monitoring, practitioner-level verification is the difference between accurate targeting and assumption.

## Our verification stack

We cross-reference our clinic-level dataset against five sources:

- **GMC** — General Medical Council, for doctors
- **NMC** — Nursing and Midwifery Council, for nurses
- **GDC** — General Dental Council, for dentists
- **GPhC** — General Pharmaceutical Council, for pharmacists
- **Save Face** and **JCCP** — voluntary accreditation registers

Each practitioner record carries the regulatory body, registration number, prescriber status, and last verification date.

## Why clinic-level data alone isn't enough

For pharma compliance under HMR 2012 Part 11, distributor due diligence under GPhC supply rules, and the upcoming licensing regime, the practitioner is the regulatory unit. A clinic-level dataset without verified practitioner records cannot support these workflows.
