## The data

22% of London skin clinics now offer GLP-1 treatments under the broader weight-management umbrella. A year ago, that figure was 8%. The category has nearly tripled in twelve months.

This article explores the trajectory, the regional pattern, and the commercial implications for injectable pharma companies tracking the UK market.

## Where it's growing fastest

Adoption is heavily concentrated in London, the South East, and Greater Manchester. Northern Ireland and rural Wales remain largely untouched. The clinics adopting GLP-1 first are those already running an active injectables practice with at least one prescriber on staff.

## Implications for pharma

For toxin and filler manufacturers, GLP-1 adoption is reshaping the commercial conversation in three ways:

1. **New visit cadence.** Patients on GLP-1 protocols return monthly, creating regular touchpoints for cross-sell into injectables and skincare.
2. **Practitioner credentialling.** GLP-1 prescribing requires GMC/NMC prescriber status, raising the regulatory bar across the channel.
3. **Competitor positioning.** Clinics offering GLP-1 are increasingly positioning as "aesthetic medical practices" rather than aesthetic salons — affecting brand partner choice.

## Methodology

This analysis draws on continuous tracking of 15,000+ UK aesthetic clinics, monitoring website content, social media signals, and clinic-verified treatment menus. Data refreshed daily.
