## The 2027 framework in brief

The Health and Care Act 2022 (Section 180) introduces a tiered licensing regime for non-surgical cosmetic procedures across England, with Scotland following with its own Non-surgical Cosmetics Procedure Bill. Implementation begins in 2027.

This piece summarises what the framework means for the 15,000 UK clinics in our dataset, regional rollout expectations, and what suppliers should prepare for.

## The three tiers

- **Red tier (highest risk):** Procedures like non-surgical BBLs being brought under CQC regulation, restricted to qualified healthcare professionals.
- **Amber tier (medium risk):** Botox, dermal fillers, similar injectables. Requires local authority licence. Non-healthcare professionals need oversight from a regulated healthcare professional.
- **Green tier (lower risk):** Procedures like microneedling. Requires practitioner and premises licence from local authorities.

## Implications for sales motion

For pharma and device suppliers, the regime fundamentally changes the buying conversation. Practitioner credentialling becomes a hard commercial filter, not a soft signal.
